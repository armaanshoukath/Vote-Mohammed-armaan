About Me

A student who believes every voice deserves a stage

My name is Mohammed Armaan Shoukath, and I'm running for Cultural Secretary at Sri Kanchi Mahaswami Vidya Mandir. Over the years, I've taken part in debates, cultural performances, and school events — and along the way, I've seen how much talent exists in our school that never gets a proper platform to shine.

I've helped organize class-level cultural activities, supported junior students preparing for their first stage performances, and worked behind the scenes on event planning for school functions. These experiences taught me that great events don't happen by accident — they happen when someone listens, plans carefully, and gives every student a fair chance.

As Cultural Secretary, I want to turn that experience into real change: more opportunities, fairer selections, and a cultural calendar that finally reflects the full range of talent our school has to offer.

My Vision

Six ideas, one stage for everyone

Every idea below is built around a simple belief — that culture at SKMSVM should belong to every student, not just a few.

⚖️

Equal Opportunities

No student should be overlooked because they're new to the stage. I want auditions and selections that give every class, every grade, and every skill level a genuine chance to participate.

🎨

Creativity

From art and music to drama and dance, our school is full of creative ideas waiting to be tried. I want to introduce open theme days and creative showcases where students pitch their own event ideas.

🤝

Teamwork

The best performances come from students working together across classes and grades. I want to build cultural committees that mix seniors and juniors so everyone learns from each other.

🌟

Leadership

Cultural Secretary shouldn't be a one-person job. I plan to create small student leads for each event, giving more students real leadership experience, not just participation certificates.

🙋

Student Participation

I want to actively invite quieter students who don't usually put themselves forward — through class visits, sign-up drives, and encouragement from cultural committee members.

🏫

School Spirit

Cultural events are where our school comes together. I want events that make every student — performer or audience — proud to be part of SKMSVM.

My Manifesto

What I promise to work on

These are concrete commitments, not just slogans. Hover over each card to see how I plan to act on it.

01

Every Talent Matters

Singing, sketching, coding, storytelling, or public speaking — I'll push for cultural events that celebrate more than just the usual dance and music acts.

02

Fair Opportunities

Clear, published criteria for auditions and selections, so every student knows exactly how decisions are made — not just who is chosen.

03

More Cultural Activities

Regular mini cultural days between the major annual events, so talent gets a stage more than once or twice a year.

04

Better Event Planning

Earlier schedules, clear rehearsal timings, and organized backstage coordination so events run smoothly and participants feel prepared, not rushed.

05

Student Feedback

A simple suggestion box — both physical and online — so every student can share ideas and concerns about cultural events directly with me.

06

Transparent Selections

Results and shortlists shared openly with participants, with brief, respectful feedback for those who aren't selected, so everyone feels heard.

The Roadmap

Where we are, and where we're headed

  1. Today

    A talented school where opportunities aren't always shared equally, and many students never get to step on stage.

  2. More Opportunities

    New event formats and fairer selections open the stage to students from every class and background.

  3. More Participation

    As trust grows, more students step forward — not just to perform, but to help plan and lead events too.

  4. Greater Confidence

    Every performance, big or small, builds confidence that carries students beyond the stage and into everyday school life.

  5. A Stronger School Community

    A culture where every student feels seen, valued, and proud to call SKMSVM their stage.
